Commercial Roofing Is a Different Animal — and We Know It.

Residential and commercial roofing share almost nothing in common beyond the basic idea of keeping water out. Commercial buildings — whether a small retail strip, a multi-unit HOA, or a large warehouse complex — present a completely different set of challenges. Flat and low-slope roofs don't shed water the way a pitched residential roof does. They rely on membrane systems, proper drainage design, and precise sealing around dozens of HVAC penetrations, vents, drains, and curbs. When any of those fail, water doesn't just drip through a shingle — it pools, backs up, and infiltrates slowly until you have a serious interior problem.

Denver's commercial roofing environment adds its own complications. Rapid temperature swings between day and night cause membrane systems to expand and contract, stressing seams over time. Rooftop HVAC units are common in Colorado's commercial buildings and create multiple penetration points that need to be correctly flashed and maintained. Flat roofs also accumulate snow load in winter, which requires attention to drainage design so melt water exits cleanly without refreezing at drains and scuppers.

We work across the full spectrum of commercial membrane systems. Whether your building has an aging modified bitumen system that needs a full replacement, a TPO membrane with failing seams, or an EPDM rubber roof that's been punctured by foot traffic, we have the crews, certifications, and manufacturer relationships to get the job done right — with minimal disruption to your tenants and operations.

Systems We Install

Manufacturer-Grade Materials. Proven in Colorado.

We're trained and certified on the commercial systems that hold up best in Denver's climate — from TPO to EPDM to standing-seam metal.

Carlisle SynTec
TPO Membrane

Thermoplastic polyolefin (TPO) is white or light grey, heat-weld seamed, and highly reflective — ideal for Denver's 300+ days of sun. Carlisle SynTec's Sure-Weld TPO is one of the most specified commercial membranes in the country, with excellent resistance to UV, ozone, and chemical exposure. Energy-efficient and available in 45, 60, and 80 mil thicknesses.

Firestone
EPDM Rubber Roofing

Firestone's EPDM (ethylene propylene diene terpolymer) is the industry's proven workhorse for low-slope commercial applications. Black rubber construction with outstanding cold-temperature flexibility — critical for Colorado winters. Seams are adhered or tape-sealed and the system handles large spans exceptionally well, making it cost-effective for warehouse and big-box applications.

GAF
Modified Bitumen

Modified bitumen is the evolution of built-up roofing — asphalt reinforced with polymer modifiers (SBS or APP) for improved flexibility and durability. GAF's commercial modified bitumen systems are commonly used for re-roofing over existing BUR systems, retail applications, and multi-family roofing where a proven, cost-effective system is needed. Torch-applied or cold-applied installation available.

Berridge
Metal Panel Systems

Berridge's standing-seam and structural metal panel systems are built for Colorado's commercial and light-industrial applications. Ideal for low-slope commercial buildings transitioning away from membrane systems, or new construction requiring 40+ year service life. Available in steel and aluminum with a wide range of Kynar 500 color finishes. Concealed fastener systems eliminate exposed penetrations entirely.

FAQ

Commercial Roofing Questions

What Denver property managers and business owners ask us most often.

Can you work around our business hours?
Yes. We coordinate scheduling upfront with your property manager, facilities team, or HOA board to minimize disruption to tenants and operations. We can work early mornings before business opens, evenings, or weekends for interior-sensitive sections of the project. We don't spring schedule changes on you — everything gets agreed to in writing before work begins.
Do you handle large commercial properties?
We handle commercial projects across the full size range — from small retail buildings and single-story office spaces up to large warehouse complexes, multi-building HOA communities, and light-industrial properties. Larger projects are scoped and phased so you're never looking at unacceptable exposure or budget surprises mid-project.
What's the difference between TPO and EPDM?
TPO is a white or light-colored thermoplastic membrane with heat-welded seams. It's highly reflective, energy-efficient, and excellent for buildings where cooling costs matter. EPDM is a black rubber membrane with adhered or tape-sealed seams. It's extremely durable, cost-effective over large spans, and performs exceptionally in cold weather. In Denver's climate both systems perform well — the right choice depends on your building size, existing system, energy goals, and budget. We'll walk you through the tradeoffs specifically for your property.
Do you provide maintenance contracts?
Yes. We offer annual inspection and preventive maintenance contracts for commercial properties. This typically includes a bi-annual inspection (spring and fall), cleaning of drains and scuppers, minor membrane repairs at no additional cost, and a written condition report each visit. Maintenance contracts extend the life of your commercial roof significantly and catch small issues before they become budget-breaking problems.

Common Issues

Common Codes
Explained

These are some of the most common diagnostic codes we see at Desert Ridge Auto. The code is just the starting point — understanding what caused it is what matters.

We Don't Just Clear Codes

Many shops clear the light and send you on your way. We identify the underlying cause and explain your options clearly — even if that means some items can wait.

P0300
Random / Multiple Cylinder Misfire

Engine is misfiring across multiple cylinders. Could be spark plugs, coils, injectors, or low compression. Left untreated, it can damage the catalytic converter.

P0420
Catalyst System Efficiency Below Threshold

Your catalytic converter may be failing. Can also be triggered by a bad O2 sensor or exhaust leak. Requires inspection to determine the actual cause before replacing a pricey cat.

P0171
System Too Lean (Bank 1)

Engine is running with too much air or not enough fuel. Common culprits: vacuum leak, failing MAF sensor, dirty fuel injectors, or a weak fuel pump.

Diagnostics
FAQ

Can't I just buy a code reader at AutoZone?
A basic code reader tells you what system triggered a light. Our professional scanner reads live data streams from every module, freeze frame data, and pending codes — giving us the full picture. Plus, we interpret the data correctly and know what to look for in Arizona's climate.
How long does a diagnostic take?
A full diagnostic scan takes about 1–2 hours depending on the vehicle and complexity. We'll call you with preliminary findings before proceeding with any repair quotes.
Is the diagnostic fee applied to the repair cost?
Yes. The $89 diagnostic fee is waived entirely if you choose to have us perform the repair. You only pay the repair cost. If you decide not to proceed, you only pay the $89 diagnostic fee.

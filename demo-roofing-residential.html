Denver's Climate Is Hard on Roofs. We Know How to Handle It.

Denver's Front Range weather is punishing — and your roof takes the worst of it. From spring hailstorms that dent shingles and strip granules, to heavy snowloads that stress decking and flashing, to intense summer UV that bakes and dries out asphalt, roofs in Colorado age faster than in most parts of the country. Add the monsoon-pattern winds that roll down from the mountains every July and August, and you have a recipe for premature roof failure if you're not paying attention.

At Peak Shield, we've spent over a decade working specifically on Denver-area homes. We know how the altitude affects material performance, which neighborhoods took the hardest hits in recent hailstorms, and what Denver building inspectors look for on permitted work. This isn't generic roofing — it's local expertise applied to your home.

Knowing when to act is half the battle. Look for missing or lifted shingles after a windstorm, granule accumulation in your gutters (it looks like dark sand), water stains on your ceiling or in your attic, soft spots when you walk a low-pitch section, or daylight visible through decking. Any one of these is a reason to schedule an inspection. The longer you wait, the more a small repair becomes a full replacement.

FAQ

Common Questions

Straight answers to the questions Denver homeowners ask us most.

How long does a residential roof replacement take?
Most residential replacements are complete in one to two days. Larger homes or roofs with steep pitches, complex valleys, or multiple dormers may take up to three days. We give you a realistic timeline before we start — and we stick to it. You'll rarely see us stretch a job unnecessarily.
Do I need to be home during installation?
You don't need to be present throughout the day. We do ask that you're available for a short walkthrough before we start — usually 15 minutes first thing in the morning — and again when we wrap up so we can walk the finished job with you. If your schedule makes that impossible, we can work around it and document everything with photos.
What happens to my old roof materials?
Full tear-off and disposal is included in every replacement job. We bring a dumpster, strip the old roofing down to the decking, inspect the decking for soft spots or rot, and haul everything away before we leave. You won't have a pile of old shingles sitting in your driveway.
Will you work with my insurance if it's storm damage?
Yes — this is one of the most common situations we work in. We're experienced with all major insurance carriers and can meet your adjuster on-site to walk the damage together. We'll document everything thoroughly and make sure nothing gets missed in the claim. We don't "chase" storms or make promises we can't keep, but we do know how to advocate for what your home actually needs.

Know the Signs

Warning Signs Your
Brakes Need Help

Your brakes communicate. If you notice any of these symptoms, schedule an inspection immediately — catching issues early can prevent costly repairs and accidents.

🔊
Squealing or Squeaking

A high-pitched squeal when braking usually means worn brake pads. The wear indicator is doing its job — don't ignore it.

💥
Grinding Noise

Metal-on-metal grinding means pads are completely worn through. This damages rotors rapidly and requires immediate attention.

↔️
Vehicle Pulling to One Side

If your car pulls left or right when braking, a caliper may be sticking or brake lines may be uneven. A safety risk that needs diagnosis.

🌀
Vibrating Brake Pedal

Pulsing or vibrating through the pedal when stopping typically indicates warped rotors — common after hard stops or heat stress.

🦶
Soft or Spongy Pedal

If your pedal sinks further than usual or feels soft, you may have air in the brake lines or a fluid leak. Do not drive — call us immediately.

💡
Brake Warning Light

The red BRAKE or ABS light on your dash signals a system fault. This could be low fluid, a sensor issue, or a hydraulic failure — get it checked.

Questions Answered

Brake Service
FAQ

How long does brake service take?
A standard pad replacement takes 1–2 hours. A full brake job with rotor replacement takes 2–3 hours. If you need more extensive work on the hydraulic system, we'll estimate the time upfront.
Do I need to replace all four brakes at once?
Not always. We replace per axle — so if your front pads are worn but rears are fine, we'll only replace the fronts. We'll show you the measurements from each corner so you can make an informed decision.
What's your warranty on brake work?
All brake service includes our 12-month / 12,000-mile warranty on parts and labor. If anything we replaced fails within the warranty period, we'll fix it at no charge.

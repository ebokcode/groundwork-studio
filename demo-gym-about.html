Our Story

Built by trainers, for people who train. Iron Valley wasn't designed by a franchise — it was built by two coaches who wanted something better.

How It Started

From Two Trainers to Scottsdale's Best Gym

In 2019, Chris Avila and Dana Park were both working as personal trainers at big-box gyms in Scottsdale — frustrated by overcrowded floors, impersonal service, and machines that were always out of order. They had a simple idea: build the gym they'd always wanted to train in.

They leased 8,000 square feet on Camelback Road, spent six months building it out by hand alongside a small crew, and opened Iron Valley Fitness in October 2019 with 40 founding members who believed in what they were building.

Six years later, they have 200+ active members, a Google 4.9-star rating, a team of four certified coaches, and a waiting list for Personal Training. They still teach classes every week and know every member by name.

Our Mission

To build a gym where every person — regardless of experience level — feels challenged, supported, and genuinely proud of what they're becoming.

Local Partnerships

We partner with local Scottsdale businesses to offer member perks — discounts at nearby restaurants, physical therapy clinics, sports massage studios, and nutrition shops. Being part of a local ecosystem matters to us.

Charity Events

Every quarter, Iron Valley hosts a charity workout event open to the entire Scottsdale community. Past beneficiaries include Boys & Girls Club of Metro Phoenix, St. Mary's Food Bank, and local youth sports leagues. No membership required to attend.

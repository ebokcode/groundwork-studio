Common Questions

Emergency FAQ

Answers to the questions homeowners ask us most after storm damage.

What should I do immediately after storm damage?
Document everything with photos before any cleanup. Don't throw anything away — debris and damaged materials are part of your insurance evidence. Call us before calling your insurance company so we can help you through the process and ensure nothing gets missed in the claim.
Will emergency tarping damage my roof further?
No. Professional tarping uses non-penetrating anchor methods that protect your roof from additional water intrusion while permanent repairs are scheduled. Improper tarping from inexperienced contractors can cause secondary damage — our crews are trained specifically for emergency situations.
How quickly can you get to my home after a major storm?
We prioritize storm response over all other work. Most Denver metro properties receive an on-site assessment within 4–8 hours during normal storm events. During major regional storm events we triage by severity and route crews to active-leak situations first — we'll give you a realistic ETA when you call.
Do I pay for the emergency inspection?
No. The emergency assessment and written damage report are completely free — even if you ultimately don't use Peak Shield for your repairs. We believe you deserve honest information before making any decisions.
Can I file an insurance claim for old hail damage I just noticed?
Colorado has a statute of limitations on storm damage claims — typically 1–2 years from the date of the storm event. If you suspect older hail damage you haven't yet claimed, contact us and your insurer as quickly as possible. We can often help identify approximate storm dates using local weather data to support your claim.
